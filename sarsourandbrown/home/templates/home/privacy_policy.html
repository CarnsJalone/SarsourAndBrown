Cookies A cookie is a message that web servers pass to your web browser when you visit Internet sites. Your browser stores each message in a small file, and when you request another page from the server, your browser sends the cookie back to the server. It is a small file that a Web site transfers to your computer to allow it to remember specific information about your session while you are connected. Your computer will only share the information in the cookie with the Web site that provided it, and no other Web site can request it. When browsing SarsourAndBrown.com, a cookie may be used to identify your browser as one that has visited the Web site before. Sarsour & Brown may also make use of memory-based cookies in support of authenticating the user of certain Web applications. Although you have the ability to modify your browser to either accept all cookies, notify you when a cookie is sent, or reject all cookies, it may not be possible to utilize Sarsour & Brown’s services that require registration if you reject cookies.
